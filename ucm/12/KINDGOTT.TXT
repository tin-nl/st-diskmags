{*f1DAS ICH - "KINDGOTT"{*f0
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

maxi single, released by Edel/Connected 1998

After three  years one  of the leading german darkwave bands released again a
new studio  album, "Egodram"... and  before all  the  maxi-CD "Kindgott". I'm
sure that at least the german friends of this kind of music know this strange
band. The problem is  that their songs  are written in german and because the
lyrics are very important  foreign people  don't got a  right idea what's the
cool side on this band. Anyway I'm writing this article in english and I hope
that some one of you is interested in open the own horizon...
At first to the contents...

o1. Kindgott
o2. Kindgott - childhood mix
o3. Kindgott - extended version
o4. Gott ist tot - Nietzsche mix

The new stuff is a bit different to older stuff from the band around the very
charismatic  singer Ackermann with  the strange  face. They  have changed the
sound and now they're  wandering on industrial ways. Real hard rhythms, cruel
noises, many squealing  and scratching  sounds are  standing in the forground
now, without  suppressing the  voice of the singer... all in one  they have a
real threatening sound created. The song "Kindgott" is a very fast industrial
song and I think  it will fill up  the dancefloors of the scene clubs without
problems. The two  mixes don't have  big differences and don't open new views
on the titletrack, but  they're good, no  question. Several  critics say that
they remember to NITZER EBB (the first mix) or NINE INCH NAILS... can't check
it out, 'coz I'm not the pure industrial  freak and don't know  every band...
haven't the money to buy all the stuff, it's really too much!
But I bought the  single because  of "Gottes Tod" or better "Gott ist tot" in
the new version. It's faster  and much  harder as  the original and I like it
very much!
Ok, just go  to your recordshop and  listen to this maxi and of course to the
full album!

Let me tell you finally only one  thing. If you like industrial music you can
buy the maxi and the album of course, even if you don't understand the german
lyrics. If you're really interested in them just translate them... and if you
like strange lyrics you should  do it! Also the old DAS ICH fans who like the
old "classic" style of  the band should check it out... it's unusual hard but
has not lost the  definite DAS ICH  features, like  the typical  voice of the
singer and several "classic" instruments are still included.
